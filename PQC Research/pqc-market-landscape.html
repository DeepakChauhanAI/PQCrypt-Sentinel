Market Structure & Business Models
Dimension Current State Implication
Market Stage Early — most enterprise buyers still in "awareness" phase. NIST 2030 deadline is now creating urgency. Major consolidation via M&A already happening (Keyfactor acquired InfoSec Global + CipherInsights in 2025; CyberArk acquired Venafi in 2023). Window to enter is 2025–2027. After 2027, market will consolidate around 2–3 dominant platforms.
Pricing Model Dominant "Contact Sales" / opaque pricing is universal for commercial tools. No vendor publishes pricing. Buyers negotiate based on asset count (certificates, nodes, endpoints). Lock-in is high once deployed. Transparent pricing and a free on-prem tier would be a meaningful differentiator in enterprise procurement.
Banking Buyer Budget Large Indian banks (SBI, HDFC, ICICI) have IT security budgets of ₹500Cr+ ($60M+/yr). PQC is a new budget line — not yet carved out specifically. CISOs are seeking education + tooling simultaneously. ₹2–5Cr ($240K–$600K) per deployment is achievable for a bank-specific PQC scanner with regulatory alignment.
Deployment Preference Indian banks will not send data outside their network perimeter. RBI data localisation requirements reinforce this. No existing commercial tool is purpose-built for Indian banking regulatory context (RBI, NPCI, SEBI). On-prem only deployment + RBI alignment is a requirement, not a nice-to-have. Creates moat vs. SaaS-first players.
Professional Services All commercial vendors bundle PS (professional services) engagement at 30–50% of product cost. Banks prefer pre-packaged tools over lengthy engagements. Self-service deployment with clear documentation is a differentiation point vs. IBM ($500K+ consulting engagement) and SandboxAQ.
Regulatory Alignment No existing product aligns with RBI guidelines, NPCI security requirements, or Indian banking compliance frameworks. All existing tools align with NIST/CISA (US), NCSC (UK), or ENISA (EU). First mover with RBI-aligned output creates a defensible local market position. Regulatory language in reports matters to Indian banking CISOs.
04 — Gap Analysis
What the Market Doesn't Cover

The white space your product needs to own. Gaps are categorised into: total gaps (no product covers this), partial gaps (covered inadequately), and commercial gaps (covered but inaccessible to your target market).

🏦
Banking Infrastructure — Total Gap
  • Payment HSM 3DES disallowance check — zero products flag this. 3DES is already disallowed yet many banks still run it on payShield/Futurex.
  • Core banking system crypto scanning (Finacle, Temenos, Flexcube) — no product has connectors for CBS vendor APIs or schema.
  • SWIFT LAU key + SWIFTNet TLS combined assessment — only partially addressed by CryptoNext/SandboxAQ, neither are banking-complete.
  • ATM EPP firmware algorithm check — entirely absent from all products. Long hardware replacement cycle means this must be flagged now.
  • RBI / NPCI interface TLS compliance — no product maps findings to Indian regulatory requirements.
  • ISO-0/ISO-3 → ISO-4 PIN block migration tracking — absent from all tools.
🔐
HSM & Key Management — Total Gap
  • PKCS#11 enumeration is only partially present in AQtive Guard and Keyfactor — no product does deep HSM firmware PQC readiness checks (Thales Luna firmware version vs. PQC support matrix).
  • KMIP protocol-based KMS scanning is absent in all tools except AQtive Guard (partial).
  • Key hierarchy mapping (master key → zone key → session key) is mentioned in CBOM spec but not implemented by any tool.
  • Offline / air-gapped root CA assessment — no tool addresses this. Root CA migration is the highest-priority step and hardest to assess.
  • Database TDE algorithm scanning (Oracle, MSSQL) — only AQtive Guard has partial support, no others.
📊
Risk Scoring — Partial Gap
  • No open-source tool does any risk scoring — all output raw findings with no prioritisation.
  • Commercial tools score risk but none implement Mosca's Theorem (data longevity vs. quantum timeline) as a first-class calculation visible to the user.
  • HNDL (harvest now, decrypt later) risk is mentioned in documentation but not surfaced as a calculated metric per asset in any tool's dashboard.
  • 5-dimension composite scoring (algorithm + sensitivity + exposure + replaceability + regulatory) exists only in SandboxAQ. All others are 1–2 dimension at best.
  • Migration priority queue — no tool produces an ordered remediation backlog sorted by composite risk score that a team can directly execute against.
🇮🇳
Indian Market — Commercial Gap
  • All commercial tools are SaaS-first or require significant cloud touchpoints — non-starter for RBI-regulated institutions where data cannot leave the bank's perimeter.
  • Pricing is $200K–$2M/yr USD — beyond the procurement comfort zone of mid-size Indian PSBs (public sector banks) and NBFCs.
  • No tool produces reports aligned with RBI IT Framework, SEBI Cyber Security guidelines, or IDRBT guidance language — compliance teams cannot use them directly.
  • No vendor has India-based support or data residency guarantees — procurement blocker for regulated entities.
  • Implementation timelines (3–6 months for Venafi, IBM) conflict with RBI audit cycles. Banks need a tool they can deploy and produce findings from within weeks.
📦
Deployment Model — Commercial Gap
  • No tool offers a single-container on-prem deployment with zero external dependencies. IBM requires Kubernetes. SandboxAQ requires cloud connectivity. Keyfactor requires 8–12 week implementation.
  • No tool has a "run once, get results" mode — all require multi-week configuration before first findings.
  • Open-source tools require significant engineering assembly — no one has combined sslyze + ssh-audit + IBM QSE + CBOM builder into a working pipeline anyone can deploy.
  • Agent deployment approval is a procurement hurdle — no tool offers a genuinely agentless path for all 7 infrastructure layers.
🔄
Remediation Workflow — Partial Gap
  • All tools stop at "here is what is vulnerable." None provide a structured remediation workflow with ticketing integration, owner assignment, and SLA tracking built in.
  • No tool tracks migration progress over time — banks have no way to measure % of assets migrated vs. the 2030/2035 deadline without building their own tracking.
  • Crypto agility recommendations (how to replace vs. which algorithm to use) are absent from open-source tools and superficial in commercial ones.
  • Vendor readiness assessment (is my Temenos version PQC-capable?) is unaddressed by all products.
05 — Verdict & Opportunity
Competitive Position & What to Build

Synthesis of the market analysis into actionable conclusions for your product.

Product Verdicts — What Each Is Good For
01
Use pqcscan / SSLyze as components
Inside your scanner engine
Don't rebuild TLS/SSH handshake scanning from scratch. Both are well-tested, open-source, and output structured JSON. Integrate them as modules inside your orchestrator. pqcscan gives you SSH + TLS PQC detection. SSLyze gives you deep TLS analysis. IBM QSE gives you source code CBOM. Assemble, don't replicate.
02
You cannot compete with SandboxAQ / Keyfactor head-on
On their turf
$5.75B valuation, DoD contracts, 5-year enterprise deals. Don't compete on general enterprise PQC scanning. Compete on the vertical they have ignored: Indian banking infrastructure, RBI-aligned compliance output, payment HSMs, core banking systems, ATM/POS layer, and on-prem-only deployment. That's your moat.
03
CryptoNext is the closest architectural reference
Study, don't replicate
COMPASS's modular architecture (open discovery layer + passive probe + analytics aggregation) is the closest to what you're building. Their COMPASS Discovery layer is open source — study the architecture. They have Banque de France as a reference — you want RBI-aligned equivalents. Their weakness: EU-only, no Indian market presence, no banking-specific modules.

Strategic Conclusion The market has two ends: free open-source tools with no risk scoring, no depth, no banking coverage — and $500K+/yr enterprise platforms built for US/EU government and Fortune 500. The entire middle is empty. A banking-specific, on-prem, RBI-aligned PQC scanner priced for Indian financial institutions has zero direct competition today. The window is 2025–2028 before the large platforms add Indian market coverage.
